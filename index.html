A Psychometric Ensemble Model for Collective Conscience

The Core Idea

This is not just synthetic data, biometrics, or a generic LLM sandbox. Instead of asking one model what a stakeholder might think, CIQ runs a stakeholder or digital twin through multiple structured moral, ethical, social, and political-psychology dimensions.

  • Moral foundations, moral development, values hierarchy, and justice orientation
  • Authority, loyalty, harm/care, fairness, purity, sanctity, and liberty sensitivities
  • Collectivism, individualism, institutional trust, empathy, and corruption tolerance
  • Pluralism tolerance, grievance sensitivity, reconciliation readiness, and identity-threat sensitivity

The agent is no longer a roleplay mask. It becomes a structured moral actor with a quantified, scenario-sensitive conscience profile.

Why This Beats Prompting

A prompt can say, "Act like a Buddhist monk, a Muslim civil-society leader, a nationalist politician, and a youth activist." CIQ instead describes each actor through a scored profile: high authority, high loyalty, medium care, low pluralism tolerance under identity threat, high sacred-value sensitivity, and low institutional trust.

The difference is measurement architecture. CIQ does not rely on a persona label; it models the moral structure behind the response.

The Unique Engine

The strongest product language is ECC Composite Engine: a multi-instrument moral-psychology scoring layer for digital-twin simulations. It can also be described as a Collective Conscience Profiler or Moral-Political Psychometric Ensemble.

This framing is safer than claiming to diagnose ECC, and stronger than describing CIQ as a chatbot or generic agent sandbox.

Critical Caveat

Synthetic ECC Is a Starting Layer, Not Proof

If everything is synthetic, investors will challenge the circularity: the AI answered psychometrics on behalf of imaginary agents, aggregated its own simulated answers, and then used that to simulate more answers.

The stronger answer is that CIQ begins with synthetic modelling for rehearsal, then progressively calibrates profiles through expert panels, stakeholder interviews, survey data, consultation transcripts, and real-world outcomes.

Synthetic data is acceptable for early scenario exploration. It is not enough for validation.

Killer Feature

Scenario-Dependent ECC Drift

CIQ does not merely classify actors. It shows how actors shift under moral pressure, identity threat, inclusive consultation, or mediator intervention.

01

Pressure Conditions

Under normal public discussion, pluralism may be high, authority medium, identity threat low, and fragmentation risk low. After a religious insult, pluralism may drop, authority may rise, identity threat may spike, and fragmentation risk may become severe.

02

Repair Conditions

After inclusive consultation, pluralism may return to medium-high and fragmentation risk may become moderate. After trusted mediator intervention, pluralism may rise again, identity threat may fall, and trust repair may become possible.
